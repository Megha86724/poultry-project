About Project

A poultry farm is an agricultural unit where birds such as chickens, ducks, and other domestic fowl are raised for the production of eggs and meat. Poultry farming plays an important role in food security, employment generation, and the rural economy because it provides a regular source of nutritious protein and income. Modern poultry farming follows scientific methods of housing, feeding, breeding, sanitation, and disease prevention to improve productivity and maintain bird health. Proper ventilation, balanced nutrition, clean drinking water, and vaccination schedules are essential for successful farm management. This project explains important poultry farm management practices through different student modules such as breed selection, feeding and nutrition, health care management, and marketing systems. It also includes field visit observations to understand practical farm operations and modern poultry production techniques.

Modern Poultry Farming Insights

🐣 Poultry farming is one of the fastest-growing agricultural sectors and contributes significantly to protein supply worldwide.
🥚 A healthy layer bird can produce around 280 to 300 eggs per year under proper management conditions.
💉 Regular vaccination and biosecurity measures help prevent major poultry diseases such as Newcastle disease and avian influenza.
🌾 Balanced feed containing protein, vitamins, minerals, and energy directly affects bird growth and egg production.
🏠 Proper housing with ventilation, lighting, and temperature control improves bird comfort and reduces stress.
